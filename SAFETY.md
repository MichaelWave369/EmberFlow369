# EmberFlow369 Safety Doctrine

EmberFlow369 exists to help people prepare, understand local risk, document evidence, and coordinate responsibly. It must never create false certainty during a life-safety event.

## Safety hierarchy

1. Life safety first.
2. Official instructions override everything.
3. Clear uncertainty is safer than fake confidence.
4. Local suggestions must be labeled as suggestions.
5. Human witness reports need provenance and confidence tags.
6. Historical receipts must not be erased when corrected.

## Emergency disclaimer

In an immediate emergency, call 911 or follow local emergency instructions. EmberFlow369 is not a substitute for emergency services.

## Evacuation language rules

EmberFlow369 may surface official evacuation language. It must not invent official-sounding orders.

Allowed local wording:

- "Prepare to leave if conditions worsen."
- "Review official evacuation routes."
- "Check county emergency channels."
- "This route appears risky based on available data. Verify with official road information."

Restricted wording unless directly sourced from an official authority:

- "Evacuate now."
- "Mandatory evacuation."
- "Road closed."
- "Shelter open."
- "Area cleared."

## Witness safety

Community witness mode must not encourage people to enter dangerous areas. The system should warn users:

- Do not drive into floodwater.
- Do not approach active fire, downed power lines, unstable slopes, or closed roads.
- Do not block emergency responders.
- Do not trespass to gather evidence.
- Do not expose private addresses, medical needs, children, or vulnerable people publicly.

## Privacy rules

Sensitive data should be minimized.

Examples of sensitive data:

- exact home address
- medical needs
- disability or mobility constraints
- children, elder, or pet/livestock locations
- phone numbers
- evacuation destination
- vehicle/license information

Default design should be local-first where possible, with explicit consent for sharing.

## Abuse prevention

The system should guard against:

- false reports
- panic amplification
- doxxing
- fake road closures
- impersonation of officials
- harassment of responders or residents
- scraping vulnerable-person lists

## UI safety rule

Any action card that includes risk should show:

- source type
- confidence
- timestamp
- whether official confirmation exists
- what the user can do safely
- what the user should not do

## Failure mode principle

When data is stale, missing, conflicting, or low confidence, the product should say so plainly and route the user toward official channels.
